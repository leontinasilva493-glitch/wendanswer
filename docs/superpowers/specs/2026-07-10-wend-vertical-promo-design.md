# WendAnswerToday 20-Second Vertical Promo Design

## 1. Objective

Create a polished 20-second product promo for WendAnswerToday.org that can be published on YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram Reels, and embedded in desktop social feeds. The video must make the product's spoiler-safe value clear within the first five seconds and end with a readable website call to action.

## 2. Audience and Message

- Primary audience: English-speaking LinkedIn Wend players who are stuck but still want to solve most of the puzzle themselves.
- Core problem: a player needs help without revealing the entire answer.
- Product promise: reveal only the amount of help needed, from one letter to a complete verified path.
- Desired response: visit WendAnswerToday.org when stuck on the daily Wend puzzle.
- Tone: upbeat, capable, reassuring, and playful rather than frantic.

## 3. Deliverable

- Duration: exactly 20 seconds.
- Canvas: 1080 × 1920 pixels, 9:16 portrait.
- Frame rate: 30 fps.
- Language: English voiceover and burned-in English captions.
- Primary export: H.264 MP4 with AAC audio.
- Preview: active HyperFrames Studio project plus representative snapshots.
- Social safety: essential copy, logo, and controls remain inside the centered 80% width and away from the top and bottom 12% interface zones.

## 4. Creative Direction

Use the approved “Solve the Stuck Moment” structure:

1. Open on the frustration of being stuck.
2. Introduce spoiler-safe help.
3. Demonstrate Reveal Letter, Reveal Word, and the verified path.
4. Turn the path reveal into the visual payoff.
5. End on a clean brand and URL lockup.

The video should feel native to WendAnswerToday instead of imitating LinkedIn. Use the site's real interface, its W path logo, and the existing grid/path visual language. Do not claim affiliation with LinkedIn or introduce unverified performance statistics.

## 5. Brand Reference

- Brand blue: `#2563EB` for calls to action, emphasis, and the logo path.
- Warm yellow: `#F59E0B` and logo yellow for hints and high-energy accents.
- Success green: `#16A34A` for completion and “streak saved” moments.
- Ink: `#111827` for headlines and high-contrast copy.
- Paper: `#F8FAFC` and white for clean backgrounds and interface cards.
- Word-path accents: `#E8572A`, `#D4449A`, `#4DBDBA`, `#98C21F`, and `#5B8DD9`.
- Typography: heavy system sans or Inter-style sans, with compact bold display copy and highly legible sentence-case captions.
- Shapes: rounded cards, circular path nodes, pill buttons, and a dark rounded device frame.

## 6. Final Voiceover Script

> Stuck on today’s Wend, but don’t want the whole puzzle spoiled? WendAnswerToday gives you exactly the help you need. Reveal one letter, one word, or the full verified path. Save your streak, skip the spoilers, and solve smarter at WendAnswerToday dot org.

The script contains 42 spoken words. Delivery should be warm, energetic, and natural, with small pauses after “spoiled,” “need,” and “path.” The final domain must remain intelligible and must not be accelerated to force timing.

## 7. Storyboard

### Beat 1 — The Stuck Moment, 0.0–5.0 seconds

- Visual: a real Wend-style grid rises into frame, slightly off-axis. A tentative path starts, stops, and recoils. The grid gives one restrained shake.
- Camera: quick push-in from 94% to 104% scale with subtle layered parallax.
- On-screen headline: `STUCK ON TODAY’S WEND?`
- Voiceover: “Stuck on today’s Wend, but don’t want the whole puzzle spoiled?”
- Sound: soft tension pulse, path scratch, and one muted error tick.
- Transition: the stopped path becomes a blue wipe that reveals the product card.

### Beat 2 — Exact Help, 5.0–8.8 seconds

- Visual: the captured WendAnswerToday interface settles into a centered phone-scale card. The answer panel and its controls receive a soft blue focus ring.
- On-screen headline: `GET EXACTLY THE HELP YOU NEED`
- Voiceover: “WendAnswerToday gives you exactly the help you need.”
- Sound: clean whoosh and a light confirmation tap.
- Transition: the focus ring contracts into the first simulated tap target.

### Beat 3 — Progressive Reveal, 8.8–13.6 seconds

- Visual: three fast, readable demonstrations. Reveal Letter uncovers one character; Reveal Word resolves one word card; the verified path control prepares the full grid reveal. A pointer or fingertip indicator appears only at the moment of each tap.
- On-screen labels: `ONE LETTER`, `ONE WORD`, `VERIFIED PATH`.
- Voiceover: “Reveal one letter, one word, or the full verified path.”
- Sound: three distinct taps with increasing pitch.
- Transition: the third tap launches the colored path-drawing animation.

### Beat 4 — Satisfying Payoff, 13.6–16.7 seconds

- Visual: colored tubes draw through the letter grid in sequence. Start nodes pop, letter tiles brighten, and the progress bar reaches 100%. Confetti is excluded; the solved path itself is the payoff.
- On-screen headline: `SAVE YOUR STREAK.` followed by `SKIP THE SPOILERS.`
- Voiceover: “Save your streak, skip the spoilers,”
- Sound: rising path tones and a restrained completion chime.
- Transition: the completed blue path curves into the brand W.

### Beat 5 — Brand CTA, 16.7–20.0 seconds

- Visual: the W path logo locks up over a white-and-yellow field. A simplified grid remains faintly behind it for depth. The domain appears large and stable for at least 2.2 seconds.
- On-screen copy: `SOLVE SMARTER` and `WENDANSWERTODAY.ORG`.
- Voiceover: “and solve smarter at WendAnswerToday dot org.”
- Sound: music resolves on a clean, positive final chord.
- End frame: no fade to black; hold the branded CTA through the final frame.

## 8. Motion and Composition Rules

- Every major visual must enter, evolve, or exit; no static screenshots placed without motion.
- Use transform and opacity animation for interface movement, plus SVG stroke drawing for the puzzle paths.
- Keep headlines to two lines or fewer and captions to two lines at a time.
- Maintain clear hierarchy: headline first, product interaction second, caption third.
- Avoid rapid full-screen flashes, excessive camera shake, and motion that obscures actual interface behavior.
- Reframe captured desktop content into focused portrait crops rather than shrinking an entire webpage into unreadable space.

## 9. Audio and Caption Design

- Voice: neutral international English, friendly adult delivery, approximately 126 words per minute across the full script including pauses.
- Music: copyright-safe light electronic pulse with no vocals, mixed beneath the narration.
- Sound effects: restrained tap, whoosh, path draw, error tick, and completion chime.
- Captions: burned in, phrase-timed to the generated narration transcript, high contrast, and positioned above the lower social-interface safety zone.
- Mix priority: narration first, effects second, music third. No sound effect may mask a spoken consonant or the domain.

## 10. Build Structure

- One master 20-second composition coordinates five beat components.
- Shared components provide the device frame, kinetic captions, simulated pointer, logo lockup, grid, and SVG path drawing.
- The website capture and local logo are treated as source assets; visual reconstruction is used only where a clean motion layer is required.
- Word-level transcript timestamps are the timing source of truth. Scene boundaries are adjusted to the final voice track rather than stretching or truncating narration.

## 11. Failure Handling

- If the narration exceeds 20 seconds, shorten pauses or tighten animation overlap before removing product meaning; never speed the final domain beyond clear speech.
- If a captured interface section is too dense at 9:16, crop to the active control and reconstruct only the surrounding neutral card frame.
- If a website asset cannot be captured, use the existing local logo and code-derived interface styling while preserving the approved message.
- If music sourcing is unavailable, deliver with narration and designed sound effects rather than use unlicensed music.

## 12. Verification and Acceptance

- HyperFrames lint and validation complete with zero errors.
- Runtime is 20.0 seconds at 30 fps, with no blank beginning or unintended black ending.
- Representative snapshots from every beat contain no clipped text, overlap, or unsafe-zone violations.
- Captions match the narration and remain readable at mobile size.
- All three progressive-help states and the colored verified-path payoff are visually understandable without audio.
- The logo and `WENDANSWERTODAY.ORG` remain readable on the final frame.
- The rendered MP4 plays correctly with synchronized H.264 video and AAC audio.
